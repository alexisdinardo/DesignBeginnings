Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

An all about me website, created using HTML, CSS, and a BootStrap template. Here I explain my relationship with design.

Logo A/B/C

Three logos made to represent me, created using three different methods: Canva, Adobe Logo Creator, and creating from scratch.

UX Testing

A study conducted to see which of the three previous logos were considered most liked. I used different UX testing tactics to ask questions and obtain data.

Cartoon Portrait

A cartoon portrait, made using Adobe Illustrator, using an actual photo of me as a base and using different tools to draw over it.

Good Design

A look into what is considered good design, created using Adobe InDesign. Here I look into details of a article color and point out how the designer uses fundamental design principles.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

Creating these projects have helped me realized how much technology affects creativity and design. Prior to this class, I have always thought of technology and creativity as their own entities. Now I realize especially with the software made by Adobe, the two coincide and work together to create most of the designs we see in the public. Being able to create art and designs in a digital space makes these designs more accessible and easily circulated. For example, if I were to draw my cartoon portrait instead of creating it on the computer on Illustrator, I would not be able to send it out and publish it at as high a quality. Making on the computer also makes the process way easier in terms of revision and changing elements. Changing elements on the computer is as easy as just dragging them across the screen whereas on paper, we would have to erase and redraw or completely start over.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Once learning about the design principles, I was able to incorporate them in my designs and projects. The three projects where this was my primary concern were the UX testing presentation, Cartoon Portrait, and Good Design. In the UX testing presentation I found myself putting an emphasis on alignment and repetition. Using repetition, I was able to make each slide unison and make sure the slide deck all blended well together. Alignment was also a concern with the text, and I made sure there was once again repetition with that to create a uniform presentation. With the Cartoon Portrait, I focused on contrast. Knowing the style of cartoon that I wanted to mimic was very “blocky,” I focused on making each section of my face/body very different colors to create a very strong contrast. I also decided to keep the poms in the photo as the real photo to add more texture and even more contrast. Lastly, with the Good Design project, I focused on looking at the good design elements of the image I chose and repeating those elements in my own design. This includes the alignment, contrast, and balance the image used.

3.Recognize and respond to ethical concerns.

By creating these five pieces, I was able to think and directly experience how technology affects ethics in design. In the website, creating alternate text to make sure the site was accessible by everyone was a main concern. Another thing to think about was the software we were creating the other pieces on. Adobe services paid, and pretty expensive, software applications that are unobtainable to every person wishing to design. Looking at other software, in this case primarily Canva, to see how to create high quality designs, that are free and require little skill was important in understanding Adobe’s role in the design community.

4. Engage with others in a workshop environment

By presenting my own work and designs, I was able to get useful feedback from my classmates. I’ve learned that by showing my work to people who have never seen it before provides a fresh look at the design and brings new ideas that I most likely never thought of. With everyone coming from different backgrounds and areas of expertise, workshopping creates a space for us all to work together and help each other both work with the technology and use that technology to help our designs look as best as possible. By looking at other classmates’ work, and by providing feedback to theirs, I was also able to learn more about design and what I think constitutes it.

5. Practice revision within and across media forms

Working with the feedback I received from my classmates and interviewees, I was able to revise and fix parts of my designs that were not translating. I’ve realized that things that look good to me, don’t always turn out the way I planned and I need to be open to changing my designs according to what other viewers say. One thing that has stuck with me from our readings was how with being a designer, particularly a UX designer, the designer’s opinion is not the one that matters, but the audience’s. The viewers, the ones that are perceiving it, are the most important when it comes to creating for the public.

6. Learn to present your work

Learning to present my work has been extremely helpful in getting used to talking through my ideas and methods. Through presenting my projects, I’ve become more comfortable with speaking in public about my own work. I have also learned how to talk through my process on how I create things and have learned to use that to help me learn more about how I decide to design things rather than just placing items on the screen that I think look good. Through this class and making these projects, I have been more deliberate about where and how I place my elements in my design.
